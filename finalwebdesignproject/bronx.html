The Bronx

More on the Borough

The Bronx is home to 1.4 million people. If it were separated from New York and became its own city,it would be the seventh largest city in the United States. The Bronx has the most green space out of all the boroughs. The largest park in New York City is actually Pelham Bay Park, three times larger than Central Park. The Bronx also has Van Corthlandt park, the New York Botanical Gardens, and the Bronx Zoo, the largest Zoo in NYC. The Bronx is also one of the most diverse counties in the United States, with a significant immigrant population. The name comes from Jonas Bronck, who owned a farm across from the river that separated the Bronx from the island of Manhattan (https://www.welcome2thebronx.com/2023/01/fun-facts-history-people-and-things-from-the-bronx-you-probably-didnt-know/#google_vignette).

Bartow-Pell Mansion

The land on which the mansion was built was initially inhabited by the Lenape. Thomas Pell signed a treaty with the Lenape for 50,000 acres in 1654. Thomas Pell’s descendant, Robert Bartow and his wife Maria Lorillard, completed the mansion in 1842. The mansion has a late Federal style exterior and Greek Revival period rooms. The family lived there until 1888, when they sold the estate to the city so it could become part of the future Pelham Bay Park (https://www.bartowpellmansionmuseum.org/ about/history/).

Gould Memorial Library

Gould Memorial Library was designed by Stanford White, who also designed the arch in Washington Square Park and the second Madison Square Garden. The library was originally built for New York University’s campus in the Bronx. The library was designed after the Pantheon in Rome. It is a circular building with a domed roof lined with 16 Corinthian columns. The dome also has an oculus at the center. There are statues of the Greek Muses, and the floor has Italian marble mosaic tile (https://www.bcc.cuny.edu/about-bcc/history-architecture/gould-memorial-library/).

Fordham University Chapel

The Fordham University Church was built in 1845 in a Gothic Revival style inspired by many European Gothic cathedrals. The church was originally built for the surrounding farm community and as a seminary chapel. The Jesuits would purchase the church in 1859, and it would later become part of the Fordham campus. The church contains an altar from St. Patrick’s Cathedral (https://sourcebooks.fordham.edu/medny/ venturi.asp).
