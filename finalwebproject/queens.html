Queens

More on the Borough

Queens is the largest borough in New York City by land mass and has the second largest population. Queens has the nickname of the world’s borough, being the most ethnically and linguistically diverse place in the world. Queens was originally the home of the Lenape people, along with other tribes on Long Island. The Dutch colonized and settled New Amsterdam, now known as New York City, in 1625, starting with what would later be known as Long Island City. In 1664, the Dutch surrendered Queens to the English. Queens would not become part of New York City until 1898 (https://queenshistoricalsociety.org/queens-history-timeline/).

Unisphere

This sculpture of the Earth is 140ft (43m). It is the focal point of Flushing Meadows-Corona Park. It was built between 1963 to 1964 and was officially dedicated in March 1964. It was built for the 1964 New York World’s Fair. Robert Moses, the head of the Parks Department, commissioned the architect Gilmore D. Clarke to design the sculpture. The planet has three rings around it, representing the three satellites that were launched into orbit. It has become an iconic symbol of Queens (https://www.nycgovparks.org/parks/flushing-meadows-corona-park/highlights/12761).

John Bowne House

The John Bowne House is the oldest home in Queens, originally built around 1661. It is the oldest remaining example of Anglo-Dutch vernacular architecture in the United States. John Bowne was an advocate for religious freedom, and his descendants would help facilitate the Underground Railroad. It is a member of the National Park Service National Underground Railroad Network the Freedom, one of a few in New York (https://www.bownehouse.org/history-of-the-house).

Flushing Town Hall

Flushing Town Hall's foundation and cornerstone were laid on June 7, 1882. The building was built with brick in a Gothic style. It has been the heart of the Flushing community. Historically, holding town halls, theater productions, and speeches. Frederick Douglass even delivered a speech there in 1865. The town hall continues to host numerous cultural events that represent the rich cultural diversity of Flushing, Queens (https://www.flushingtownhall.org/mission-and-history).
